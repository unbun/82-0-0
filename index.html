How the simulation works

Every number comes from real NHL stats (pulled from the league's official API). Stats untracked in older eras (shots before 1959, hits/blocks before 2005, goalie save% before ~1955) are imputed by tier and shown in muted gray.

Your six picks produce two rates using all six stats per player:

xGF = (Σ skaters: GPG + ½·APG) · playmaking_balance · shot_volume
      + physical_bonus + D_offense_bonus − PIM_cost

xGA = base − slope · (sv_quality + D_PPG·0.48
      + allSkaters_BLK·0.22 + allSkaters_HIT·0.038 − PIM_penalty)

Forwards' HIT and BLK contribute to defense — physical play wins puck battles in all three zones. Elite offensive D add an xGF bonus beyond their rawScoring share (power-play QB, breakouts, zone entries). A playmaking balance factor penalizes all-sniper or all-playmaker lineups (assists should outnumber goals ~1.8:1 in real NHL play). PIM costs you on both sides.

Then 82 games are simulated: each draws goalsFor ~ Poisson(xGF) and goalsAgainst ~ Poisson(xGA). The draws are seeded from your exact roster — same lineup always returns the same record. Going 82-0-0 requires elite stats at every position, a high-SV% goalie, and some Poisson luck.
