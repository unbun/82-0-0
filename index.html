How the simulation works

Every number comes from real NHL stats (pulled from the league's official API). Players are bucketed by franchise and decade; skaters are ranked by points/game, goalies by games played. Stats the NHL didn't track in older eras (shots before 1959, hits/blocks before 2005, goalie save% before ~1955) are imputed by tier and marked with a ~, so old eras aren't unfairly helped or hurt.

Your lineup turns those stats into expected goals per game:

scoring = Σ over your 5 skaters of (goals/game + ½·assists/game)
xGF     = -0.10 + 1.0 · scoring                 (goals you score)

defense = (goalie save% - .86)·22 + D-pair quality
xGA     = 4.73 - 1.20 · defense                 (goals you allow)

A skater played on the wrong side for their shot hand (off-side) is discounted ~18%. Then 82 games are simulated: each draws goalsFor ~ Poisson(xGF) and goalsAgainst ~ Poisson(xGA) — more goals win, fewer lose, equal ties. The draws are seeded from your exact roster, so the same lineup always returns the same record. Going 82-0-0 takes a near-perfect, balanced roster with an elite goalie.
